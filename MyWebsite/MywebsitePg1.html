What Type Of Owner Should Be Matched?

Ever Hear A Dog Looks Like Their Owner?

Well every guinea pig personalities should match their owners. There are different species of guinea pigs and they all have different tempermants. If you are looking to adopt a guinea pig the breed should be matched with the age, and personality of their owner to ensure a perfect match. Below I will show you pictures of each guinea pig and give a decription of what you could picture everyone you know with their soulmate of guinea pig!

  • American: EASY CARE: The most common breed, lively, affectionate, easy to care for, smooth and sleek coat. This pet can be adopted by a loving family with children
  • Abyssinian: EASY CARE: Mischievous, loving, fun, rosettes in the coat. Should be maintained by an adult
  • Peruvian: MODERTE CARE:Curious, alert, kind, long and silky coat. Should be cared for by an older child with a very chill tempermant
  • Silkie: BOSS LEVEL CARE: Charming, gentle, calm, long and smooth coat. Needs to be cared for by a quiet home and home body teen or adult. Elders can also be a very great fit!
  • Teddy: HARD TO CARE FOR: Social, lively, friendly, dense and wiry coat. This breed should be cared for by anyone any age but young children should be closely monitored.
  • Texel: MODERATE CARE: Friendly, elegant, graceful, long and curly coat. Experienced Guinea pig owners are more likely to choose this breed. Should not be cared by children
  • Himalayan: MODERATE-HARD LEVEL OF CARE: Similar to Siamese cats, calm, sweet, short and smooth coat with dark points. More experienced owners
  • Skinny: BOSS LEVEL CARE: Hairless except for some fuzz, active, playful, sensitive to temperature.This breed should be cared for by a person that has time to manage their skins needs. Not for children. This guinea pig looks like a baby hippo right? AWWWWW
